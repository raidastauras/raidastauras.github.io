I built an app that creates adventure trips. Adventyr's goal is simple - turn simple intent - _e.g. I want to explore Montenegro for 6 days and I love: nature, hiking, bunkers, old factories, etc._ - into an actual route with places to visit. Pick a country, how many days you have, and what you’re into — castles, bunkers, canyons, museums, military sites, whatever.

Here's what the app does under the hood:

1. scans the entire country using the Google Maps API for each interest you select. That sounds easy until you realize Maps is noisy, inconsistent, and often returns “close enough” instead of “correct.” So I deliberately over-collect. Then an LLM steps in to aggressively filter out the junk, keep only what truly matches your interests, and generate short, readable descriptions for every place. What you see in the app is already the result of multiple passes of machine + model sanity checks.

2. All returned places go back to the backend, get clustered into geographic regions, and are fed—again—into an LLM. This time the job is selection and optimization: pick the best spots, balance density vs. distance, and produce a route that actually fits your time window without turning your vacation into a death march.

That’s the idea. Not a trivial thing to build; challanging to make sure each part works good - prompts, LLM loops, proper filtering, and figuring out the UI.
