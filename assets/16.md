I built an app that turns a few clicks about what you like into an optimized adventure trip. Adventyr's goal is to turn simple intent - _e.g. I want to explore Montenegro for 6 days and I love: nature, hiking, bunkers, old factories, etc._ - into an actual route with places to visit. Pick a country, how many days you have, and what you're into - castles, bunkers, canyons, museums, military sites, whatever - the app will do the rest.

Here's what the app does under the hood:

1. Scans the entire country using the Google Maps API for each interest you select. It should be easy but Google Maps is noisy and inconsistent so I got to do a couple of tricks to make sure it works okay-ish. Then it uses an LLM to filter out the junk, keep only what truly matches your interests, and generate short descriptions for every place. What you see in the app is already the result of multiple passes of machine + model sanity checks.

2. Once it finishes exploring the space of all possible places worth a visit, then all the places go back to the backend, gets clustered into geographic regions, and are fed again into an LLM. This time the job is selection and optimization: pick the best places, balance density vs. distance, and produce a route that actually fits a time window without turning a vacation into a death march.

That’s the idea. Not a trivial thing to build. Challanging to make sure each part works good - prompts, LLM loops, filtering, dealing with Google Maps API, and figuring out the UI.
