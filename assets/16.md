I built **Adventyr**, an app that turns users travel intent into an actual route with places to visit. For example, say you want to explore Montenegro for 6 days and love nature, hiking, bunkers, and old factories - Adventyr generates a route that fits. Just pick a country, set your trip length, and choose your interests - castles, bunkers, canyons, museums, military sites, old factories, you name it - and the app handles the rest. You get the idea - if you want to visit a country and do what you like, there's so many things you could see and routes you could take. Adventyr is here to generate you these routes - you can generate as many as you want until you find the one.

Here's what the app does under the hood:

1. Scans the entire country using the Google Maps API for each interest you select. It should be easy but Google Maps is noisy and inconsistent so I got to do a couple of tricks to make sure it works okay-ish. Then it uses some tricks and an LLM to filter out the junk, keep only what truly matches your interests, and generate short descriptions for every place. What you see in the app is already the result of multiple passes of machine + model sanity checks.

2. Once it finishes exploring the space of all possible places worth a visit, then all the places go back to the backend, gets clustered into geographic regions, and are fed again into an LLM. This time the job is selection and optimization: pick the best places out of the huge pools of places found, balance place density vs. geo-distance, and produce a route that actually fits a time window and is geniunly exciting.

That’s the idea. Challanging to make sure each part works good - dealing with Google Maps API, filtering, prompts, LLM loops, and figuring out the UI. I love it and hope I will be able to pull this off and turn it into a decent service.
