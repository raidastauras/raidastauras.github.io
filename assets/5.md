I worked at a tiny company doing government consulting projects. We had no time to manually track government procurement notices for relevant opportunities — I wanted to automate it. Built a web scraper that monitors all tenders, filters them by our criteria, and sends notifications.

That also meant I needed a frontend. Tried Django, didn't like it, ended up with Vue.js. Explored hosting options (AWS, Google Cloud), and after a bunch of iterations, had my first proper full-stack app — frontend, backend, database.

Not the most efficient way to learn web dev. You could just, you know, study it properly. But where's the fun in that?
